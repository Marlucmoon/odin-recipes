Pad Thai

Pad Thai recipe that truly stacks up to great Thai restaurants

Pad Thai is a Thai noodle stir fry with a sweet-savoury-sour sauce scattered with crushed peanuts. It’s made with thin, flat rice noodles, and almost always has bean sprouts, garlic chives, scrambled egg, firm tofu and a protein – the most popular being chicken or prawns/shrimp.

A note about the magic Pad Thai sauce :

Traditional pad thai sauce includes fish sauce, vinegar, sugar and tamarind paste. Tamarind paste is not easily found at a regular grocery stores so I substituted rice vinegar. If you would like to use tamarind paste, substitute about 2 Tablespoons in place of the rice vinegar in this recipe. I also added a big scoop of peanut butter to my sauce, because I think it gives the whole dish an added creaminess and boost of flavor that I love.

Ingredients :

  • The magic sauce
  • Noodles
  • Sauté Protein
  • Eggs

Preparation info :

30 Minutes from start to finish, perfect for a busy weeknight!

Steps :

  1. Cook Noodles following package instructions
  2. Make sauce : stir together fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar (or tamarind paste), Sriracha, and peanut butter, if using. Set aside.
  3. For the Sauté protein Heat 1½ tablespoons of oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp, chicken or tofu, garlic and bell pepper. Cook until meat is done.
  4. Cook Eggs by pushing everything to the side of the pan. Add a little more oil and add the beaten eggs. Scramble the eggs, breaking them into small pieces with a spatula as they cook.
  5. Combine and Toss : Add noodles, sauce, bean sprouts and peanuts to the pan (reserving some peanuts for topping at the end). Toss everything to combine.
  6. Serve: Top with green onions, extra peanuts, cilantro and lime wedges. Serve immediately!
