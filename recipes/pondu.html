Congolese Pondu

Pondu or saka saka, is a very popular Congolese dish made from cassava leaves.

Here is the recipe for delicious Congo vegetable stew. The dish is traditionally eaten with rice as an accompaniment or as a main course with sardines in oil

Ingredients (3 people) :

  • 1 sachet of cassava leaves (approx. 500g)
  • 1 chilli
  • 3 African eggplants
  • 1 medium zucchini
  • 1 leek
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 smoked fish soaked and deboned (or a can of pilchards)
  • olive or Palm oil
  • salt pepper

Preparation info :

Preparation time is 10 minutes. The cooking time is 1h30

Steps :

  1. Peel and chop the onion
  2. Peel and chop the aubergines and zucchini
  3. Put the pondu in a saucepan and add a little water and cook it with a chilli (be careful not to pierce it) for 30 minutes.
  4. Add the aubergines and zucchini cut into pieces, the minced or crushed onion, the minced leek, the fish and a few tablespoons of palm oil.
  5. Season and cook for at least 45 minutes to 1 hour, then stir. The pondu must be very tender.
  6. Serve with rice, fufu or plantains.
